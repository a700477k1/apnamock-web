Privacy Policy

At ApnaMock, we value your privacy. Our platform has been designed to function primarily within your own web browser. Unlike many online services, ApnaMock does not require you to create an account or submit personal information in order to access mock tests.

Most of the information generated while using the website remains stored locally on your own device. This allows you to continue your preparation, revisit your results, and resume unfinished tests without requiring an online account.

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time whenever new features or services are introduced. Continued use of ApnaMock after such updates indicates your acceptance of the revised policy.

Information Stored Locally

  • Your selected theme (Light/Dark mode).
  • Quiz progress for unfinished tests.
  • Your completed test results.
  • Your attempt history.
  • Your saved favourite questions.
  • Your generated custom tests.

Cookies & Browser Storage

ApnaMock makes limited use of browser technologies to improve user experience.

  • Cookies may be used by future third-party services such as Google AdSense.
  • Local Storage is used to remember user preferences and quiz data.
  • IndexedDB is used for storing larger offline datasets such as favourites and attempt history.
  • No browser storage is used to collect sensitive personal information.

If Google AdSense or similar advertising services are enabled in the future, those providers may use cookies in accordance with their own privacy policies.

Offline Data Storage

One of the key features of ApnaMock is that much of your learning data is stored directly inside your own browser instead of being uploaded to a remote server.

This enables:

  • Offline-friendly quiz experience.
  • Automatic recovery of interrupted tests.
  • Fast loading without repeated downloads.
  • Personal revision through favourite questions.
  • Attempt history even without user accounts.

Because this information is stored locally on your device, clearing your browser data, uninstalling your browser, or using another device may remove this information unless future cloud-sync features are introduced.
